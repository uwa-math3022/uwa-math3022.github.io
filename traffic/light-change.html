Initial conditions

\gdef \Vmax {V_\mathrm{max}} \gdef \Nmax {N_\mathrm{max}} \gdef \Fmax {F_\mathrm{max}}

Cars lined bumper-to-bumper wait at a red traffic light which turns green at t = 0. In symbols,

\eval{N}_{t = 0} = \begin{cases} \Nmax, & x < 0 \\ 0, & x > 0 \end{cases}.

What happens?

Animation

  • Characteristics in purple
  • Dense/slow regions in red
  • Sparse/fast regions in blue

Characteristics

Dense region

First consider t = 0, x < 0, i.e. the left half of the x-axis, where the cars are waiting at full density. Here we have

\begin{aligned} N &= \Nmax \\ V &= 0 \\ \frac{\td F}{\td N} &= -\Vmax. \end{aligned}

Thus the characteristics are straight lines with slope -\Vmax, and so the left half of the x-axis extends into a triangular region with full density N = \Nmax.

Empty region

Next consider t = 0, x > 0, i.e. the right half of the x-axis, which is initially empty. Here we have

\begin{aligned} N &= 0 \\ V &= \Vmax \\ \frac{\td F}{\td N} &= +\Vmax. \end{aligned}

Thus the characteristics are straight lines with slope +\Vmax, and so the right half of the x-axis extends into a triangular region with zero density.
