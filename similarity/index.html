Problem

Consider an infinite block of density \rho, specific heat capacity c, and thermal conductivity k.

Suppose the block is initially at zero temperature throughout. At time t = 0:

  • Cram thermal energy per area Q into the plane x = 0

What happens?

Q. How can you put a non-zero amount of thermal energy into a plane which has zero thickness?
A. It's an idealisation, like a point charge. In the case of a point charge, the charge density is infinite but the amount of charge is finite. Here, the initial temperature of the plane x = 0 is infinite, but the energy per area is finite.
